Literature Survey: Assistive Technologies

Visual impairment affects millions globally, impacting autonomy and access to essential services. Our literature survey explores the latest advancements in assistive technologies, highlighting the gaps and challenges in areas such as fall detection, medication identification, and public transport navigation. The need for holistic solutions that integrate these technologies is evident, aiming to foster independence and safety for visually impaired individuals.

Key areas of focus in our research include:

  • Enhancing real-time fall detection to improve safety and prompt response.
  • Utilizing AI for medication identification through visual recognition.
  • Developing inclusive public transport navigation aids to ease commuting.
  • Examining the role of voice-guided and haptic feedback for improved accessibility.

Research Gap

Despite technological advancements, few comprehensive solutions are available to assist visually impaired individuals with everyday tasks such as navigation, medication management, and public transportation. Current tools often address single-use cases, lacking a cohesive framework that integrates these essential functions.

Our research addresses this gap by developing an all-in-one assistive solution that empowers visually impaired users through a single platform, enhancing accessibility and independence in diverse environments.

  • Integrating fall detection, medication identification, and transport navigation into one application.
  • Using voice guidance and haptic feedback for real-time accessibility.
  • Ensuring ease of use through a streamlined, user-centered design.

Research Problem

Visually impaired individuals often face limitations in their ability to move independently and access essential resources. Traditional assistive technologies tend to lack integration, making it challenging for users to access multiple features in a seamless manner. This research aims to address these limitations by creating a unified platform tailored to the diverse needs of visually impaired users.

  • Creating a holistic assistive solution for navigation, fall detection, and medication management.
  • Improving accessibility with voice-guided and haptic feedback mechanisms.
  • Reducing reliance on multiple devices or apps by centralizing functionality in one application.

Research Objectives

The primary objective of our research is to develop a user-centered application that empowers visually impaired individuals by offering a cohesive solution for navigating their environment, managing medications, and ensuring safety. This application will integrate multiple assistive functionalities, enabling users to access all necessary resources within a single platform.

  • Developing a unified platform with navigation, fall detection, and medication management capabilities.
  • Enhancing user independence through intuitive, voice-controlled interactions.
  • Ensuring safety in various environments with real-time assistance and fall alerts.

Technologies Used

Our project integrates a variety of cutting-edge technologies to provide a robust, responsive, and accessible solution for visually impaired individuals:

  • YOLO Model: For real-time object and obstacle detection, enhancing navigation safety.
  • OCR (Optical Character Recognition): To identify bus route numbers and medication labels for easier access to information.
  • TensorFlow: Deep learning framework used for training and deploying models, such as fall detection and movement classification.
  • React Native: Frontend framework for developing a responsive, user-friendly mobile interface.
  • Flask and FastAPI: Backend frameworks for handling API requests and processing real-time data.
  • MQTT (Message Queuing Telemetry Transport): Lightweight messaging protocol used for real-time data communication between IoT devices and applications.
  • Node-RED: Flow-based development tool for integrating device data and creating dashboards for monitoring and control.
  • Google Maps API: For navigation assistance, providing route and location data.
  • Firebase: To securely store user data and images, enhancing data accessibility.
  • Twilio API: For sending notifications to guardians or caregivers as needed.

Features

  • Real-Time Fall Detection

    This feature uses motion sensors and machine learning to detect falls instantly, sending alerts to designated contacts and providing audio feedback for user assurance.

  • Public Transport Assistance

    Integrates with OCR and AI to identify bus route numbers, guiding users in boarding the correct public transport with voice instructions for enhanced independence.

  • Injury Detection and Notification

    Monitors physical activities and detects falls or injuries, notifying guardians or emergency contacts when necessary to prioritize user safety.

  • Medication Identification

    Employs camera-based AI to identify medications from scanned images, enabling visually impaired users to manage their prescriptions with confidence.

Real-Time Fall Detection

This feature utilizes advanced motion sensors and machine learning algorithms to detect falls in real-time, immediately notifying guardians or emergency contacts upon detection. The system also provides audio feedback to the user, confirming detection and prompting them for a response. This proactive safety feature offers additional assurance, ensuring timely assistance during potential accidents.

Public Transport Assistance

The app assists users in identifying public transport options through advanced OCR and computer vision technologies, enabling them to detect bus route numbers and confirm the correct transport. Real-time guidance and voice prompts ensure a seamless and confident transit experience, empowering users to travel independently.

Injury Detection and Notification

This feature uses machine learning to identify potential injuries or falls and promptly notify guardians or emergency contacts. Real-time alerts ensure that assistance can be rendered quickly, providing peace of mind to users and their families. Additionally, voice guidance aids users in assessing the severity of minor injuries independently.

Medication Identification

Leveraging OCR and image recognition, this feature enables users to scan medication labels, identifying the correct medicine and dosage. The app provides audio feedback to confirm medication, enhancing safety and ensuring users adhere to their prescribed routines independently.
